A Life Built on Choices

Every moment presents us with a choice. Whether to rise early or sleep in, to speak or stay silent, to act or remain still - these decisions shape not just our days, but our very souls. We often drift through life unconsciously, letting circumstance and habits dictate our path. But there exists a more deliberate way of living.

C.S. Lewis captured this profound truth when he wrote, "Every time you make a choice you are turning the central part ofyou, the part that chooses, into something a little different than it was before." Each decision, however small, molds us into something new. Over time, these countless choices accumulate into the architecture of our character, slowly transforming us either toward heaven or away from it.

Many of us live reactively, responding to life's pressures without true awareness. We follow what feels natural or easy, unaware that these passive choices still shape us. Yet the path to a meaningful life demands more - it requires us to live actively and intentionally. To pause before each decision, however small, and choose consciously rather than drift unconsciously.

This deliberate living isn't just about the big decisions - career changes, relationships, moral crossroads. It's about the tiny choices we make every day: whether to smile at a stranger, to give time to prayer, to resist a small temptation. Each choice either strengthens or weakens our capacity to choose well in the future. Each decision either builds habits of virtue of patterns of vice.

The beautiful paradox is that by consistently choosing the harder, more intentional path, we gradually make it easier. Each good choice creates momentum toward the next one. We become what we repeatedly do. Through conscious decision-making, we slowly forge ourselves into creatures of purpose, capable of living in harmony with God, others, and ourselves.
