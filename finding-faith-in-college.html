Finding Faith In College

When I arrived at The Catholic University of America, I was Catholic in name only. Though baptized, faith had been more of a family tradition than personal conviction - something done rather than something lived.

I spend most of my life keeping God at arm's length, driven by a mixture of fear, pride, and uncertainty. Like many young people, I reacted defensively to authority and direction. How ironic that I let this rebellious spirit keep me from the very source of true freedom. Yet God is patient, and sometimes He plants us exactly where we need to grow.

At Catholic U, I found myself surrounded by people who lived their faith authentically. Through countless conversations and question, I began to recognize a deeper yearning - I wasn't looking for simple answers, but for God Himself. What I thought was intellectual curiosity was actually a soul's deep yearning for its Creator. The walls of pride began to crumble as I realized that submitting to God wasn't losing myself, but finding who I was meant to be.

At the time I write this, I'm in OCIA at the university.

This is just be beginning of my journey home to the Church, but already I can see how God used my time here to draw me closer to him.
