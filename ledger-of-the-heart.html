The Ledger of the Heart

We focus obsessively on what enters us - crafting careful checklists of church attendance, Bible reading, charity, and prayer. We mark each box with diligence, as if holiness could be achieved through perfect attendance. Yet Jesus points past these to what emerges from within: evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false testimony, slander. These are not external impositions but internal revelations.

The transformation Christ offers inverts this entirely. When God reshapes our hearts, He doesn't eliminate our disciplines but reorients them. The checklist remains - we still gather, pray, give, and study - but it transforms from a ledger of righteousness into a map of grace. Where once we marked boxes to prove our worth, we now engage these practices as channels through which our transformed hearts express their new nature. What flows from within changes: wisdom replaces evil thoughts, love replaces hatred, faithfulness replaces adultery. This is no mere behavioral adjustment but a fundamental rewriting of our deepest desires.
